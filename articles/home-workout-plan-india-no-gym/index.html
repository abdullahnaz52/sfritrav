Complete Home Workout Plan for Indians (No Gym, No Equipment)

📅 2026-04-16 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ SfriTrav Editorial Team

You do not need a gym membership to get fit. The human body is a complete resistance training machine — and some of the world's best athletes built foundational strength through bodyweight training. Here is a structured 4-week plan that works in any Indian home.

Week 1–2: Foundation (Beginner)

Perform this circuit 3 times per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday) with a rest day between sessions. 2 rounds, 30 seconds each exercise, 10 seconds rest between.

  • Wall push-ups: Builds chest, shoulders, triceps with low joint stress
  • Chair squats: Sit and stand from a chair — knee-friendly introduction to squats
  • Standing marching: Cardiovascular warm-up, hip flexor activation
  • Superman holds (10 seconds): Lower back and glute strengthening
  • Plank (10–20 seconds): Core stability

Rest 2 minutes between rounds. Total time: 15–20 minutes.

Week 3–4: Building (Intermediate)

Progress to 3 rounds. Increase to proper push-ups, full squats, and hold planks for 30 seconds. Add jumping jacks between exercises for cardiovascular benefit.

  • Push-ups: Full body on knees if needed — progress to full
  • Bodyweight squats: Feet shoulder-width, weight through heels
  • Reverse lunges: Step back, lower knee toward floor — safer than forward lunges
  • Glute bridge: Lie on back, drive hips up — essential for lower back health
  • Plank: 30–45 seconds
  • Mountain climbers: 20 reps — full body cardiovascular and core

Nutrition for Home Workouts

Eat a small meal or snack with carbohydrates and protein 1–2 hours before training. After training, eat within 30–45 minutes. Dal, eggs, paneer or curd with roti or rice is a perfectly good post-workout meal. Hydration: 3–4 litres of water on training days.
